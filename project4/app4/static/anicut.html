Built by: Chola King Karikalan
Era: Around 2nd century CE
Purpose: Irrigation — to divert water from the Cauvery River to the fertile delta regions of Tamil Nadu


Material: Made of unhewn stone (granite blocks)

Length: Around 329 meters

Height: About 5.4 meters

Design:
It’s a diversion dam, not meant to store water but to regulate and divert it
Water is diverted into canals leading to the delta plains — some of the most fertile in India
